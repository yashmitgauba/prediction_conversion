# Conversion Prediction using Machine Learning

## Overview

This repository contains the research paper **“Predicting Conversions using Machine Learning”**, which explores how machine learning and causal inference techniques are transforming modern digital advertising systems.

The study focuses on conversion prediction, uplift modeling, contextual decision systems, delayed feedback handling, and privacy-aware optimization methods used in real-world advertising platforms.

The research highlights how modern advertising systems can move beyond traditional attribution-based models and adopt more adaptive, incremental, and data-driven optimization strategies.

## Abstract

Conversion prediction has become a core problem in digital advertising and marketing analytics. Traditional systems mainly optimize attributed conversions, but they often fail to measure the actual impact generated by advertisements.

This research reviews various machine learning and causal inference approaches used in conversion optimization systems. The paper examines predictive modeling techniques, uplift modeling, contextual bandit systems, delayed feedback learning, and privacy-aware measurement frameworks.

The study also discusses operational challenges such as sparse data, delayed conversions, calibration drift, and privacy-related signal loss in advertising environments.

---

## Research Objectives

The primary objectives of this research are:

- To study machine learning models used in conversion prediction systems
- To analyze the limitations of attribution-based optimization
- To compare predictive learning with causal uplift modeling
- To understand adaptive optimization using contextual bandits
- To examine delayed feedback and privacy-related challenges

## Comparative Analysis of Models

| Model Category | Examples | Strengths | Limitations |
|---|---|---|---|
| Classical Models | Logistic Regression | Simple and interpretable | Limited interaction learning |
| Tree-Based Models | XGBoost, LightGBM | Strong tabular performance | Weak sequence understanding |
| Deep Learning Models | DeepFM, DIN, DCN | Learns high-order interactions | Requires large datasets |
| Funnel-Based Models | ESMM | Reduces selection bias | More complex architecture |
| Delay-Aware Models | Delayed Feedback Models | Handles delayed conversions | Higher system complexity |

# Attribution vs Incrementality

## Attribution-Based Systems

Traditional systems optimize observed conversions but often ignore actual causal impact.

### Limitations

- Over-targeting existing converters
- Weak causal understanding
- Poor budget efficiency

---

## Incrementality-Based Systems

Incrementality-focused systems estimate how much an advertisement actually influences user behavior.

### Advantages

- Better ROI optimization
- Improved targeting quality
- More accurate ad impact measurement

# Delayed Feedback Challenges

Delayed conversion feedback is one of the biggest challenges in advertising systems.

A user may convert:

- Minutes later
- Hours later
- Days later

This creates problems such as:

- Incorrect labeling
- Delayed optimization
- Bias in learning systems
- Unstable model updates

The paper discusses delay-aware learning frameworks and survival-analysis-based approaches for handling delayed feedback.
